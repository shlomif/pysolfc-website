Royal Parade

Picture Gallery type. 2 decks. No redeal.

Object

Move all cards to the foundations.

Rules

Royal Parade is a variation of Picture Gallery Similarly, the layout consists of three rows of playing piles, a row for newly-dealt cards, and a castoff pile for Aces.

All Aces are cast off to the pile on the right. Use the <A> key. When you clear a space on the tableau, you can only fill it with the right card:

  • In the first row, you build up sequences starting with a Two,
  • in the second row with a Three, and in the
  • third row with a Four.
Additionally, the position of two cards on the tableau can be swapped if both of them would end up in the correct row as a result.

You build up sequences incrementing by three, up to the face cards. Thus, in the first row, each pile is 2-5-8-Jack, in the second row 3-6-9-Queen, and in the third row, 4-7-10-King. Once a sequence has been started, you have to follow suit.

If you clear a space at the bottom it will get automatically filled with a card from the talon. If the talon is empty, clear spaces in the talon cannot be filled.
